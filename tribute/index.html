Linus Torvalds

The Architect of Open Source — Architect of Linux and Git, shaping the modern digital world

Milestones in Engineering History

1991

The Birth of Linux

While studying at the University of Helsinki, he posts a famous memo announcing a free, hobbyist operating system kernel, laying the foundation for modern enterprise infrastructure.

1999

The Commercial Era Evolution

Major enterprise tech companies rally behind the Linux kernel, cementing it as the global standard for web servers, supercomputers, and back-end cloud architectures.

2005

Inventing Git

Frustrated with existing source control options while managing Linux development, he designs and builds Git in just a few weeks, fundamentally changing how engineers collaborate on code globally.

Present

The Invisible Engine

Today, his work powers over 90% of cloud workloads, all Android smartphones, the world's fastest supercomputers, and the version control platforms used by every developer.
