Milestones in the Evolution of Coins

600 BCE

The Lydian Lion

The first official coins are minted in the Kingdom of Lydia (modern-day Turkey) using Electrum, a natural gold-and-silver alloy, stamped with a royal lion emblem.

500 BCE

Punch-Marked Coins of India

Ancient Indian Janapadas begin issuing silver bars and pieces stamped with distinct symbols like suns, trees, and animals, marking the birth of independent coin minting systems in Asia.

336 BCE

Hellenistic Portraiture

Alexander the Great revolutionizes coin design by placing realistic human portraits of rulers and mythological deities onto silver tetradrachms, spreading artistic realism across the globe.

1662 CE

Milled Coinage Machinery

The transition from crude hand-hammered blanks to highly precise machine-milled edges is perfected, introducing reeded safety edges to eliminate the fraudulent clipping of precious metal.
